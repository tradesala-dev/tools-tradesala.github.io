Frequently Asked Questions

Tradesala recommends 1000×1000px in a 1:1 square ratio. Minimum accepted is 800×800px. File size must be under 5 MB, minimum 80 PPI. The primary product image must have a clean white background.

Common rejection reasons: blurry or low-resolution image, non-white background on the primary image, file over 5 MB, non-square ratio, product covering less than 90% of the frame, watermarks or text overlaid on the image, and images below 800×800px.

Completely safe. All images are processed entirely in your browser using the HTML5 Canvas API. Nothing is uploaded to any server. Your files never leave your device — which also means this tool works fully offline after the page loads.

Bulk mode supports up to 100 images per batch. All processing is local — no server needed. Download all resized images as a ZIP, or download approved-only images separately.

80–92% JPEG quality is recommended. This keeps files under 200 KB (ideal) while maintaining visual sharpness. Below 75% can cause visible artefacts that risk rejection. PNG ignores the quality slider — it is always lossless.

No — the primary (main) product image must have a pure white background (#ffffff). Coloured, gradient, textured, or lifestyle backgrounds are not permitted for the main listing image on Tradesala. Additional or secondary images (image slots 2–8) may include lifestyle shots, infographics, and coloured backgrounds to showcase context and features.

Text overlays, promotional banners (e.g. "50% OFF", "Best Seller"), watermarks, brand logos, and borders are not allowed on the primary image. They are an explicit rejection reason. Secondary images may include size charts, feature callouts, and usage instructions as infographic overlays. Keep the primary image clean — product only on white.

For most categories, the product should be photographed outside its packaging so buyers can clearly see what they are purchasing. Exceptions include sealed or packaged consumables (food, cosmetics, supplements) where the sealed pack itself is the product. For multi-piece sets, all items should be arranged clearly within the 1:1 frame.

Three common methods: (1) White sweep/backdrop — place product on white chart paper or fabric near a window with natural light. (2) Lightbox — a portable photo lightbox (available for ₹500–₹2,000) gives professional results on a budget. (3) Background removal software — shoot on any background and remove it using tools like Remove.bg or Canva. Ensure no shadows fall on the background.

Yes — modern smartphones (12MP and above) produce images that fully meet e-commerce guidelines when shot correctly. Key tips: use the rear camera (not selfie), shoot in good natural light or with a lightbox, use a tripod or stable surface to avoid blur, shoot in the highest resolution setting, and avoid digital zoom. Most smartphones today output images well above 1000×1000px.

PPI (pixels per inch) is the digital screen measure — Tradesala requires a minimum of 80 PPI. DPI (dots per inch) is technically a print measure but often used interchangeably with PPI. Resolution (e.g. 1000×1000px) is the total pixel count — what actually matters for online listings. A 1000×1000px image at any DPI setting will display the same on screen. Focus on pixel dimensions, not DPI metadata.

Tradesala allows up to 8 images per listing. Best practice: Image 1 — primary white background shot. Images 2–3 — different angles (front, back, side). Image 4 — lifestyle/in-use shot. Image 5 — size chart or dimensions infographic. Images 6–8 — feature callouts, close-up details, packaging. Listings with 6+ images consistently see higher conversion rates.

Yes — directly and indirectly. Tradesala's listing quality score factors in image completeness and quality, which influences search ranking. High-quality images also improve click-through rate (CTR) and conversion rate, both of which are signals that boost organic ranking over time. Listings with all 8 image slots filled and a high-resolution primary image consistently outrank thin listings.

JPEG — best for most product photos. Smallest file size, fastest loading, universally supported. Use at 85–92% quality. PNG — use only when transparency is genuinely needed (e.g. logos, products with irregular shapes). File sizes are 3–5× larger than JPEG. WebP — best compression ratio but slightly newer; fully supported by all modern browsers. Recommended if you want the smallest possible file at the same quality. All three are accepted by Tradesala.

Aspect ratio is the relationship between an image's width and height. Tradesala requires a 1:1 square ratio (equal width and height, e.g. 1000×1000px). Non-square images get auto-cropped or letterboxed by the platform, which cuts off parts of the product and looks unprofessional in search grids. Always crop and pad to 1:1 before uploading — this tool does it automatically by placing your image on a white square canvas.
